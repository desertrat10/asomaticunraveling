# Somatic Experiment #1 — The Event Experiment
## Boulder, July 12–14, 2026

---

## Working Thesis (v0.1)

**Autonomic headroom, not trigger type, determines time-to-cliff.** Two 6-hour outings in a 48-hour window, two distinct cascade pathways — visual-proprioceptive and auditory-trigeminocervical — converged on the same endpoint. The variable that compressed collapse from ~45 minutes to minutes was remaining system headroom at trigger onset. Every controllable element of both events got pre-addressed by design; the one unplanned variable (a room conversion) produced the fastest cascade. The experiment demonstrates that a compromised nervous system can re-enter demand environments — but only when the outing treats the event as a fraction of the architecture and the infrastructure as the majority.

**Secondary thesis (surfaced in composting — closes the post):** the same somatic signals this experiment learned to read in real time function as the body's exit instructions from environments that harm it — and an entire wellness industry now trains people to override them. See §9.

## Outline

### 1. Why this experiment
- First demand-environment test since round-2 PT began — five months out
- Environment selected for controllability: low stakes, no performance obligation, community that already understands the condition (no social masking cost), trusted social anchor on site
- Frame: this reads as a designed experiment with a hypothesis, controls, a go/no-go gate, and a pre-identified cut — not a "getting back out there" story

### 2. Protocol stack — what deployment actually required
- **Capacity banking (T-minus 24h+):** deep-sleep support, overnight K-tape to prevent spasm escalation, deliberate couch-rest as banking, not fatigue response
- **Pre-load sequence before departure:** yin yoga, ice, CBD, percussion, foam roller
- **Pain management timed to demand phase, not symptom onset:** first micro to the drive, second micro to crowd arrival — dosing as headroom protection, not rescue
- **Transit as known load class:** route, parking, and post-drive decompression all pre-addressed
- **Buffer architecture:** ~2 hours of event inside a 6-hour outing; the other 4 hours were resets, transitions, and exit design
- **Exit architecture:** red-flag signal → pre-briefed anchor → quick goodbye → immediate environmental change

### 3. Event 1 — outdoor, ~30 people, 95°F
- Clean entry (rested, pre-loaded)
- Trigger: circular socializing — cervical rotation outpacing the proprioceptive system; a close-in conversational partner tipped an already-compromised stack
- Red flags: visual disruption, word loss mid-stream
- Cliff at ~45 minutes of dense standing conversation; pre-load held ~1.5 hours total
- Immediate ANS settling on environmental exit — the reset pattern itself as data

### 4. The recovery gate
- Tuesday ran as a go/no-go decision gated by Monday's log
- Checkpoint criteria: pain trend stable, body-scan trend improving, autonomic signals clearing to background
- Gate cleared → second test proceeded

### 5. Event 2 — indoor, 100+, staged architecture
- Entered pre-loaded (poor sleep + stacked demand hours before trigger)
- Staging: coffeeshop reset, low-demand social buffer, seated panel, controlled 1-1 networking (circle trigger deliberately avoided)
- The uncontrolled variable: unplanned room conversion — noise floor escalated fast
- Cliff in minutes, not 45 — different pathway (auditory-environmental, trigeminocervical nucleus already loaded from screen exposure)
- New signal: ear ringing at cliff — now reclassified as a cervical signal with earlier unrecognized presentations
- Second new mechanism: speech-speed corruption syncing with room energy — a distinct warning class from Event 1's word loss

### 6. The cut
- A third event (long drive, remote, no easy exit) got cut before the data required it
- The decision came from design logic, not crisis — the protocol working as designed

### 7. Findings
1. Headroom, not trigger type, sets time-to-cliff — pre-load state compressed 45 minutes to minutes
2. Two distinct cascade pathways produce the same endpoint — the system has multiple entry doors and one floor
3. The ear-ringing reclassification extends the trigeminocervical signal timeline backward — past data now reads differently

### 8. Cost accounting
- Delayed cost arrived on the known 6–48 hour pattern (bilateral activation ~13 hours post-cliff)
- The body paid the expected cost; the data justified the expense
- Candidate for claims registry: K-tape/SCM→temporal relief, pending confirmation through the next log cycle

### 9. What surfaced in composting — the ahimsa thesis
- The experiment trained real-time reading of somatic exit signals. Composting the second cliff surfaced a thread the project had been circling without naming: **somatic recognition of toxic spaces**
- The critique: yoga and wellness practices, ported into corporate infrastructure, now help people calm their nervous systems in order to *stay in* the containers harming them — regulation deployed to override the body's exit instructions rather than honor them
- The ahimsa question: what role do practitioners play in perpetuating harm when the skill they transmit lets labor endure a system designed to extract from it?
- The reframe: maybe the work runs the other direction — not helping people self-regulate inside destructive containers, but helping them get so connected to their own somatic signals that they exit
- This thesis emerged from the same body, in the same 48 hours, that generated the experimental data. The experiment taught the reading; the composting revealed what the reading is for.
