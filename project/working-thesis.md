# A Somatic Unraveling: Field Notes from the Cremation Grounds
## Project Thesis & Architecture

**Phase:** Experimental — gathering
**Status:** daily field notes + structured recalibration, documented through the HealingOS

---

## Thesis

*A Somatic Unraveling* documents what happens to selfhood when the body-as-production-tool fails permanently — and what a person builds in the wreckage. The author spent thirty years deriving identity from physical output: competitive athletics, physically demanding trades, documentary fieldwork carrying heavy gear through post-conflict terrain. Six degenerative cervical discs and a destabilized nervous system ended that mechanism of self-definition. The project investigates the ontological question underneath the medical one: when the body rejects the producer's tooling, what remains?

The answer takes the form of a quest narrative (Frank's typology, *The Wounded Storyteller*) anchored by three methodological pillars, each drawn from a different knowledge tradition the author holds:

1. **Nervous system knowledge** — traceable through science, anatomy, published research, and structured self-experimentation; grounded in a bachelor's degree in Yoga Studies and clinical collaboration with a treating care team.
2. **Wounded storytelling as seva and jana yoga** — medical narrative as a genre practiced as yogic service: connecting to the other through embodied storytelling, with Frank's witnessing framework supplying the scholarly lineage and the author's tantric lineage supplying the ethical structure.
3. **HealingOS + composting** — sovereign patient data infrastructure that restores the health narrative from medical colonization; the patient as data architect and keeper of her own story and care. The patient also uses a seperate "AI as Yantra" to compost the emotional landscape of her chronic condition and intense periods of flares and physical therapy.

These three pillars map directly onto the author's 25-year mission frame — Humanity × Technology × the Mystical. The author did not plan this; the archive confirms it is the first of her creative projects where all three run through the same work at once.

**One line:** the memoir and the infrastructure are the same material observed from two angles — field notes from the final assignment, the one the documentarian can't leave.

## The Dual Output

The project produces two artifacts from one practice:

| Output | Form | Audience |
|---|---|---|
| **The manuscript** | Literary auto-theory / medical narrative | Readers inside chronic conditions; the narrative medicine field; general literary nonfiction |
| **The data companion** | Open patient-generated dataset + HealingOS methodology, published openly | Patients building their own sovereignty infrastructure; open-health and DeSci communities |

Almost no illness narrative can credibly apply across both the literary and the open-data worlds at once. This one lives in both. The data companion also functions as a concrete reference implementation for how a personal knowledge OS connects to a shared methodology layer.

**Manuscript model:** the writing phase produces fresh, original writing. The field notes serve as primary-source reference to what happened in real time — not as text to assemble. Public-facing excerpts demonstrate finished writing but do not preview finalized book content.

## Genre Position

Auto-theory (the Nelson / Boyer / Preciado lineage) with a structural inversion: the theoretical frame is not borrowed from the library — it is the survival mechanism itself. The condition created the problem; the problem forced the infrastructure; the infrastructure is the theoretical contribution. No register shift is required between "here is my pain" and "here is my analysis," because the analysis is embedded in the pain that produced it.

Witness literature: precise documentation of experience the available scripts don't contain, where fidelity itself is the disruptive act. Fragmentary memoir built by composting: daily capture → long-pattern recognition → architecture emerging from the material, not imposed on it.

The company, for companionship rather than template: *The Undying* (Boyer); *The Two Kinds of Decay* and *Ongoingness* (Manguso); *Pain Woman Takes Your Keys* (Huber); "On Being Ill" (Woolf); *Giving Up the Ghost* (Mantel).

## Phase Architecture

### Phase 1 — Experimental

The gathering. Daily field notes continue. The recalibration experiments run with clinical collaboration: traditional (orthopedics, PT) and holistic (yoga therapy, craniosacral) modalities documented through the HealingOS with the same rigor across the board.

Produces: the daily field-note record (the book's primary source), the structured experiment dataset (the data companion's core), and a live public experiment.

**Phase gate, not phase date:** the experimental phase closes when the recalibration protocols stabilize into a largely self-administrable regimen — not when the calendar says so. `body_pace > timeline` governs the phase boundary itself.

### Phase 2 — Writing

Fresh manuscript composition from the field-note record. The field notes remain the reference layer; the manuscript is written new. The daily practice continues in whatever form the body permits — the practice preceded the book and outlasts it.

### Phase 3 — Publishing

Begins when the proposal package exists: proposal, polished chapters, and one fully executed triptych as formal proof-of-concept. Stands on a finished body of work.

## The Triptych — Signature Formal Structure

Three temporal layers of one witness, connected by the same tissue.

| Layer | Source | What it holds |
|---|---|---|
| The image | documentary archive | what the eye saw |
| The original field note | raw notes from the making | what the body experienced while the eye saw it |
| The present-tense Field Note | the daily practice | what that body has become |

One incident per triptych, as chapter breaks or interstitial sections. The image is evidence, not illustration; the caption carries the tax. The kit is material, not backstory: the heavy cameras, the load pattern laid down over decades — now being excavated by bodywork from the exact tissue that carried it.

## Thematic Threads

- **Language of the broken.** Pain vocabulary was built for acute injury — cause, scale, trajectory. There is no grammar for *permanent, not dying, not improving, still here.* The linguistic isolation is the wound inside the wound.
- **The management layer and its bypass.** The learned translation of experience into what others can metabolize — and the way precision of description drops that layer for writer and reader at once.
- **Healthcare navigation in chronic pain.** The granular texture policy writing can't reach: short appointments, prior authorizations, performing pain correctly to get the right referral, serving as your own case manager on compromised capacity. The HealingOS as the infrastructure answer.
- **Body as altar, neck as oracle.** Theory produced inside the fire, not about it from safety. The cremation ground as laboratory; the nervous system as the current working ground.
- **No retroactive editing.** The work was worth it AND the cost is permanent. Grief for capacity, not for choice.
- **Sovereignty progression, not recovery arc.** What movement looks like when healing is off the table: the condition holds, the infrastructure versions.

---

## Public Infrastructure — Boundaries

1. **The held-back rule.** The strongest portion of the raw material never touches the public layer. It belongs to the book. Decided per-note at sanitization time; when in doubt, held back.
2. **Sanitization is a human act.** The writer performs every sanitization pass. No AI edits the raw field notes, ever.
3. **Two publishing homes, kept distinct.** Finished literary writing publishes to a human-voice space. The machine-assisted layer — weekly pattern reads and body-data summaries — publishes here, openly labeled as what it is.
4. **Provider privacy.** Patterns and trends only; providers referenced by role, never by name; no raw clinical data in public artifacts.
